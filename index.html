Hi there, I'm Aleksei Petrenko

Making computers do things at which, at the moment, people are better. (Rich and Knight, 1991)

Hi, I'm a computer scientist from Nizhny Novgorod, Russia.
I am currently working on 3D scanning and human digitization projects at the computer vision startup itSeez3D.

My goal is to understand intelligence better and to incorporate this understanding into the design of the next generation of AI systems.

Research interests

I am interested in a broad range of machine learning and AI topics. So far, I've been focusing on deep learning, computer vision and software development in general, but my main inspiration is a study of intelligent agents. I'm particularly curious about the following fields of contemporary AI research:

  • Hierarchical RL: task decomposition, AI systems that use multiple spatial and temporal resolutions
  • Self-motivated exploration, curiosity
  • Long-term planning with sparse reward
  • Emergence of communication between the AI agents
  • Using simulations to train AI

Personal project: Deep RL in a gridworld called "MicroTbs" (2017)

A work in progress: github.com/alex-petrenko/rl-experiments
An OpenAI gym-compatible 2D environment and some RL algorithms trained in it: Double DQN, A2C. Inspired by an old game Heroes of Might and Magic III. The game itself is quite challenging for contemporary AI. I designed this tiny environment to resemble some of the features of the original game: scouting, different terrain, picking up resources, collecting an army. Personal project: "4D" video (2016)

github.com/alex-petrenko/4dvideo
Using my 3D-scanning-expertise I programmed a "4D" grabber and player for Intel RealSense and Google Tango. The player is based on modified Guibas-Stolfi triangulation algorithm and can generate 3D mesh in realtime (300fps on PC, 100fps on Android). With this software I captured a lot of cool 4D clips:

4D video (R200 #01, with texture) by apetrenko1991 on Sketchfab
